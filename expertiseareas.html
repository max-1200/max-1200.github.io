Expertise areas
Below my personal development during my bachelor will be explained. Especially the parts that are not yet explained by the showcase pages. In the showcase pages, my most important projects are listed and in here a reflection on my development can be found.
Creativity and aesthetics
Even before my study, I have always been interested in this expertise area. Since I was a child I have always liked drawing, this was also one of the reasons I wanted to study industrial design. It’s just one of the factors that in combination with other interests made me want to study industrial design. During these studies I have learned a lot more about the creative ideation process. Like physical Low fidelity prototyping during aesthetics of interaction.

During the bachelor it has also been one of the expertise areas that I focussed on the most. I developed my sketching abilities with the exploratory sketching course. After this, by applying it in the projects I participated in. Even more with my participation as a designer in the student team TU/ecomotive. What I also learned during my bachelor's is that sometimes you need more than a rendered drawing to show something in a realistic scenario or for a presentation. This is why I also learned to work with adobe photoshop and blender to make photorealistic renders to create convincing scenes of ideas. I especially developed myself on this during the USE line light pact case. Where the case was to design an interactive lighting installation for district-e, the new station area. See the image the right original images from [1]. Additionally, I used and further developed these skills in my role as a designer for TU/ecomotive. Especially the ability to render drawings and quick sketching was developed to a higher level than what I was able to do before. During my FBP this development translated to almost exact previews of what I was going to create and low-fidelity prototypes. It confirmed my ability to use these skills in the ideation process and the ability to create the matching aesthetics and features I had in mind.

technology and realization
Along with creativity and aesthetics, this was one of the other expertise areas which made me choose industrial design as a study. Along with drawing I have always been interested in how things are made and I enjoy the process of figuring out thinking up solutions for turning imagination into reality. From the start of my bachelor, I have enjoyed this expertise area a lot. Starting at the realization of the product during from idea to design. However, I have developed a lot of skills that I did not have at the beginning of my bachelor's. One of the main ones is being able to create CAD models for prototyping. Although there was no course for this that thought the skill of CAD modeling. I developed this skill gradually during engineering design, project 3, aesthetics of interaction, and especially during TU/ecomotive.

Realizing prototypes with various production methods has always been a goal during my bachelor's. To develop this I took up a service desk assistant job at TU/e innovation space. Here I learned the ins and outs of 3d printing. For FDM as well as SLA printing. Also, laser cutting was a newly acquired making skill I learned here. Outside of the hard skills I learned at innovation space, I also learned quick problem-solving skills when it comes to making. Students come in with specific problems all the time. The repetitive brainstorming about making problems learned me invaluable lessons about the making process. At TU/ecomotive I learned to even move a step beyond prototyping. I learned the reality of working with large-scale 3d printing. The difficulties it brings but also the benefits it brings. When it comes to electronics I have learned throughout my study how to apply electronics in prototypes. First with the course creative electronics. But after this by applying my knowledge in project 02 and in my final bachelor project.

Math data and computing
I think this expertise area is one that goes best while seeing it in context with technology and realization for me as an industrial designer. I developed myself the most in this expertise area when it comes to programming. Additionally to following the course and creative programming where I learned to code with processing and the basics of Arduino. I also developed myself further because this skill means to me that I can turn more ideas into reality. Which is an important aspect for me in a project.

Outside of this course and creative electronics for programming I did not feel that I got the whole picture of what coding means in combination with electronics. But also when it comes to how software development is structured. Therefore, as an extracurricular activity, I educated myself through the internet on how open-source software can be used and created on Github. But I also learned the basics of raspberry pi, coding with C and through a Domestika course on creative coding with javascript [2] and other web development videos on Youtube. With this knowledge, I challenged myself to create this portfolio website without a framework. Outside of this, I feel I learned a lot about applying mathematics with programming. With the application of magnets and hall effect sensors to realize the tangible day planner, the derivative of the incoming sensor data needed to be used. Not only did I figure out that this was necessary, but I was also able to apply it inside the program.

When it comes to data I have always felt that it wasn’t my strong suit. Just because I am not really interested in the process of analyzing it because the application would mostly be a table or a graph. Which I get is important. Yet this does not motivate me intrinsically. However, the courses Making sense of sensors and intelligent interactive products showed me that there are also times when data gathering is needed for the functionality of a product. This helped me be more motivated when it comes to data gathering. Being able to apply data to improve functionality for me is a way better motivater than just analyzing data to draw conclusions.

User and society
Although user and society was not my favorite expertise area at the start of my bachelor's is one that I explored mainly in project 02. In this project, I chose to be more involved with user testing in the form of taking interviews and helping to analyze the data from these interviews. This process showed me the importance of this side of the project. And thought me how to apply what I learned about interviewing in user-centered design to a project. I continued this development by preparing a semi-structured interview for project 3 too. As well as taking some of these interviews to figure out how people felt about the concept of the shape-shifting vehicle.

One part of this expertise area that I do really believe in is looking at the bigger picture of societal opportunities. Trying to come up with problems that are on a larger societal level. For example, connecting the projects I participate in with problems I see on a societal scale. This is why joined as a member of TU/ecomotive among other things. Because I did not feel that I was getting enough experience working on something you believe in during the projects of industrial design. At TU/ecomotive we looked the large-scale environmental impact cars have and tried to create a car that solved these problems. Like my Final bachelor project in which I try to tackle the problem of unrepairable consumer electronics devices. This topic was right along with my vision for the future and met the values I have as a designer.

Business and entrepreneurship
From the start of the beginning of the study I felt that this expertise area was the hardest to explore. The courses I chose were not focussed on this expertise area because at the beginning of my bachelor's. Because my interests were at different places. However, in the second year, I was looking for this development since I felt this was a part I was not developing. This was where the development of SWZ Weekboek came in perfectly to learn how it is to work with a client outside of a project structured by the university. From this experience, some invaluable lessons were learned.

At TU/ecomotive I learned a lot from working in an interdisciplinary team. This was the first time when I participated in a project at that time where everyone was working towards a predetermined vision and goal with each other. Even though it’s not a business it learned me a lot about the boundaries you need to set when it comes to the work you do and when to recognize that you are doing too much. Not only this it only thought me a lot about responsibility in the team and the importance of communication between members. Because everyone in the team is dependent on steps done by other members. During the projects you do during the bachelor, this is mostly not the case. When you have to finish a car before a press presentation deadline this certainly becomes a bigger factor. Looking back this pressure was a great learning experience when it comes to dedication.

Another invaluable lesson was the process of getting certain parts manufactured. Dealing with partners was an invaluable lesson. The biggest lesson is that there needs to be clear communication between the employee on the production floor who is going to produce your designs. A lot of time can be wasted when the formats of files, the method of production, and the timeline are not properly communicated. Additionally, in my third year, I participated in the course design innovation methods. However, filling in business was mostly just filling in business canvasses. Which showed me the importance of framing the business idea you have. However, I do not see this expertise area as one I am done developing. With some of my favorites like creativity and aesthetics, technology and realization, and math data and computing I do feel that I have a certain structure in how I do things. However, when reflecting on this expertise area and user and society I still feel that I’m searching for a good way to apply my abilities and have a structure in which I use them in a project.
